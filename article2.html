Inspirational Leadership

Servant Leadership

I was interviewed this afternoon by the Channels Television news crew on the socio-psychological aspects of the recent spree of looting across our country. Mr. Kelvin, the reporter, asked me what I felt is the true push behind the looting as he pointed out that many of the observed looters are not the poor we often refer to. In answering I brought to his notice that the issue is not the 'hunger in the land' narrative but a deeper problem.

A great proportion of our population exist in a state of disconnect with this entity called Nigeria, not because they do not believe that they are Nigerians but due to their inability to locate themselves as integral parts of this entity. Nigeria talks about them but Nigeria has failed to talk to them. Nigeria is not listening to them. Therefore their thinking revolves around sorting out their own issues personally from day to day without expecting much, if anything, from the state. They know they only become relevant every four years as the election cycle revolves.

Finding out therefore, in the midst of a turbulent period that 'palliatives' meant for them have been stored away by the same leaders who have disregarded them was a trigger. The tendency to be selfish, just like the leaders was stoked and they reacted in the distasteful manner that we observed. We witnessed a display by our compatriots that left us gobsmacked.

Seeing people who can more than afford the items they joined in to loot in the fray simply reveals the 'free for all' and 'I must get my share' mentality borne by the greater part of our people. This state of affairs is what the spectacle of leadership we have has resulted in.

At a time like this servant leadership is a major part of the way forward. The question then has to be asked whether the people in place today can practice servant leadership in its true essence. Leadership is meant to inspire the best potentials of any society and to incubate the emergence of the skills and talents of the people for individual and collective success.

Leaders eat last.
